\noindent\textbf{Goal.}
(1) Understand biological correlates of MAL detectable in peripheral
plasma. (2) Develop a blood-based proteomic risk score to enrich studies
for MAL and emphysema progressors.

\noindent\textbf{Outcome.}
Emphysema progression = change in CT lung density Phase~3 $-$ Phase~2
(\texttt{Change\_lung\_density\_vnb\_P2\_P3}, HU). Negative = worsened.

\subsection*{A. Volcano Plot (protein $\sim$ MAL)}

For each of 4,979 proteins, the model is:
\[
  \log_2(\text{protein}) \;\sim\;
  \beta_{\text{MAL}} \cdot \texttt{meanmal}
  \;+\; \text{age} + \text{sex} + \text{race}
  + \text{pack-years} + \text{smoking}
  + \text{scanner} + \text{PC1--PC5}
\]
$\hat\beta_{\text{MAL}}$ tells us: for a one-unit increase in MAL,
how much does the log\(_2\) protein level change, after adjusting for
the listed covariates? FDR correction (Benjamini--Hochberg) across all
4,979 tests.

Each point is one protein. $x$-axis: MAL $\hat\beta$.
$y$-axis: $-\log_{10}(\text{FDR})$.
Red = FDR $<$ 0.05 and enriched; blue = FDR $<$ 0.05 and depleted;
grey = not significant. Top 20 by FDR are labelled.

\textbf{Key result.} 45 of 4,979 proteins were significant at FDR $<$
0.05.

\section*{Proteomic Risk Score --- Methods and Results}

\textbf{Data split.} 70/30 train/test (\texttt{set.seed(2024)}),
$n_{\text{train}} = 914$, $n_{\text{test}} = 392$.

\textbf{Step 1 --- Log2 transform all protein RFU values:}
\[
  \tilde{x} = \log_2\!\bigl(\max(x,\; 10^{-6})\bigr)
\]
Transforms skewed RFU values to an approximately normal scale.
The $10^{-6}$ floor prevents $\log(0)$ errors.

\textbf{Step 2 --- Adaptive weights from ridge regression:}
\[
  w_j = \frac{1}{|\hat\beta^{\text{ridge}}_j|}
\]
A ridge regression is first fit on the residualised MAL outcome
(after projecting out the unpenalised covariates). Each protein $j$
gets a weight inversely proportional to its ridge coefficient.
Proteins that ridge already identified as informative get
\emph{lower} penalty in the LASSO step; irrelevant proteins get
\emph{higher} penalty. This gives the adaptive LASSO its oracle
property --- it tends to select the true model.

\textbf{Step 3 --- Adaptive LASSO selection:}
\[
  \min_{\boldsymbol\beta} \left\|
    \mathbf{y} - \mathbf{X}\boldsymbol\beta
  \right\|^2
  + \lambda \sum_j w_j |\beta_j|
\]
$\lambda$ is chosen by 10-fold cross-validation ($\lambda_{\min}$).
Covariates (age, sex, race, smoking, pack-years, scanner, PC1--PC5)
are assigned $w_j = 0$ so they are never penalised.

\textbf{Step 4 --- Post-selection OLS refit.}
The selected proteins are refit by ordinary least squares.
LASSO shrinks coefficients toward zero, so the LASSO $\hat\beta$
values are biased. OLS on the selected set gives unbiased estimates,
proper standard errors, confidence intervals, and $p$-values.

\textbf{Step 5 --- Compute the risk score for each subject:}
\[
  \text{PRS}_i = \sum_{j \in \mathcal{S}}
    \hat\beta_j^{\text{OLS}} \cdot \tilde{x}_{ij}
\]
where $\mathcal{S}$ is the selected protein set.
The PRS is then standardised to mean~0, SD~1 across the full cohort
so that the regression coefficient has units of HU per SD of the
score.

\textbf{Result.} 378 proteins selected. Test $R^2 = 0.157$.

\section*{Table 2 --- Causal Mediation}

\noindent Pathway tested: MAL $\to$ Proteomic Risk Score $\to$
Emphysema Progression. Fitted using the \texttt{medflex} R package
(natural effects framework) with robust sandwich standard errors.

The idea is to decompose the total effect of MAL on progression into:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{NDE} --- how much of the MAL effect operates through
    pathways \emph{other than} the protein score (the direct path)
  \item \textbf{NIE} --- how much operates \emph{through} the protein
    score (the mediated path)
\end{itemize}

Two models are fitted internally by \texttt{medflex}:
\[
  \text{Imputation model: }
  \Delta\rho \;\sim\; \texttt{meanmal} \times \text{PRS} + \text{covariates}
\]
\[
  \text{Natural effects model: }
  \Delta\rho \;\sim\; \texttt{meanmal}_0 + \texttt{meanmal}_1 + \text{covariates}
\]
$\texttt{meanmal}_0$ is the MAL value at which the mediator
(PRS) is set (direct path); $\texttt{meanmal}_1$ is the actual MAL
value driving the outcome (indirect path). The difference between them
isolates the mediated portion.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption*{\textbf{Table 2.} Natural effects model results.}
\begin{tabular}{lrrr}
\toprule
Effect & Estimate (HU) & $z$ & $p$ \\
\midrule
Natural Direct Effect (NDE)   & $+19.72$ & $2.92$ & $0.004$ \\
Natural Indirect Effect (NIE) & $-0.12$  & $-0.04$ & $0.971$ \\
Total effect                  & $+19.60$ & ---    & ---     \\
Proportion mediated           & \multicolumn{3}{c}{$-0.6\%$} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\textbf{Key result.} MAL has a large, significant direct effect on
emphysema progression (NDE $p = 0.004$). The proteomic risk score
mediates essentially none of this effect (NIE $p = 0.97$, proportion
$= -0.6\%$).
